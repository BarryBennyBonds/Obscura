Mausoleum At Halincarnassus

The mausoleum was constructed in 352 BC and was destroyed by an earthquake in 1404 AD. The mausoleum was built as a tomb for Mausolus, who was a governor in the Persian Empire(His name is also now the eponym for all mausoleums). Mausolus's widowed wife Artemisia (who was also his sister, however this was considered customary at the time) commissioned well known Greek artchitects to design the tomb. The mausoleum was nearly 14 stories tall and square, with the base being more than 10000 square feet. At each side of the tomb stood nine massive columns, and at the top was a stepped pyramid which stood by a four horse marble chariot where there were images of Mausolus and Artemisia.

Modern historians believe that two years would not be enough time to build a structure of that size and complexity leading many to believe that construction began before the death of Mausolus or was continued by later rulers.

The structure is believed to have been destroyed an earthquake around 1402, however it should be noted that at the time, local Greeks, and Turks had no name, or legends about the ruins, which possibly suggests an even earlier destruction.

The Colossus Of Rhodes

The colossus itself was a statue of the Greek sun god Helios, constructed in the city of Rhodes (on the island of the same name) in 280 BC. The statue was constructed to celebrate the succesful defence of Rhodes against an attack by Demetrius I of Macedon, who had besieged the city for a year with a large army and navy. Most contemporary descriptions of the Colossus place it's height to be approximately 70 cubits (33 meters or 108 feet), which places the ancient statue to be near the height of the Modern Statue of Liberty (from feet to crown, not including base).

However the statue did not stand for long as it was destroyed by an earthquake in 226 BC, with the statue snapping at the knees and the remains being broken up and sold for scrap in 654 AD after Arabian forces raided Rhodes and transported the bronze to the east to be melted down.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria

The lighthouse was constructed by the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Ancient Egypt during the reign of Ptolemy II Philadelphus (280-247 BC). The lighthouse was estimated to be at least 100 meters of 330 feet tall and for many centuries was one of the tallest man-made structures in the world.

Between the years 956 AD - 1323 AD the lighthouse was severely damaged by 3 earhquakes and became abandoned, however much of the structure remained somewhat in tact until 1480 AD, when the last remnants of the structure were used to build the Citadel of Qaitbay on the same site.
